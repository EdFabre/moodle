Before You Take the Practice Exam

This practice exam is intended to help you prepare for the SAS Base Programming Certification exam. To help you take the practice exam effectively, this page provides tips on

  • preparing for the practice exam
  • differences between the practice exam and the SAS Base Programming Certification exam
  • taking the practice exam and saving your results.


Preparing for the Practice Exam

Before you take the practice exam, you can review relevant concepts in lessons, courses, or libraries from the SAS self-paced e-learning catalog on the Web at support.sas.com/selfpaced.

When you submit the practice exam, you receive recommendations about specific lessons that you can use for review.




Differences between the Practice Exam and the SAS Base Programming Certification Exam

This practice exam is constructed to test the same knowledge and skills as the SAS Base Programming Certification exam. The practice exam tests the same concepts as the Certification exam, and all topics are equally weighted between the two exams. In addition, all test items on both exams are multiple-choice, single-answer questions.

However, you should be aware of the following differences between the practice exam and the Certification exam:


Feature Certification Exam Practice Exam
number of questions 70 questions 50 questions
time limit 2 hours no time limit, but 85 minutes is equivalent to 120 minutes on the production exam
questions per page 1 question per page (can scroll forward and back through questions) all on one page (can scroll up and down through questions)
countdown timer one-minute countdown timer for each question, to show you how long you spend on each question no timer



Taking the Practice Exam and Saving Your Results

Although there is no time limit on the practice exam, you must complete it in one browser session. Your answers will not be saved if you close your browser.

If you must close your browser before you have submitted your practice exam, you should print the exam or write down the answers that you have completed. You will need to enter these answers again the next time you open the exam.

If you want to save your results in order to reference them later, you should print the feedback page after you have submitted your practice exam and received your score.




Successful performance on the practice exam does not guarantee your performance on the Certification exam. Best of luck. And thank you for your interest in the SAS Certified Programmer Program!
