Disconnecting from the DBMS

Finally, to end the connection to the DBMS, use the DISCONNECT statement.


General form, DISCONNECT statement:
DISCONNECT FROM dbms-name | alias;

where dbms-name | alias identifies the database management system or its alias.

If you omit the DISCONNECT statement, an implicit DISCONNECT is performed when PROC SQL terminates.


Example

In the PROC SQL step below, the DISCONNECT statement ends the connection to the DB2 database that has the alias Dbshare, and the QUIT statement terminates the SQL procedure. 

     proc sql;
        connect to db2 as dbshare (ssid=db2a);
        ...
        disconnect from dbshare;
     quit;
