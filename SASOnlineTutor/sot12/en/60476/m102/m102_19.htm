Creating One-Way Frequency Reports

Now let's begin thinking about your next report. Remember that one of the reports that you need for the board meeting is a frequency report that shows

  • the number of orders for each product category
  • the percentage of total orders for each product category
  • the number of orders from each supplier
  • the percentage of total orders for each supplier.

A one-way frequency report describes your data by showing the distribution of a variable's values.

The report that follows shows the distribution of orders across all product categories. Frequency is the number of orders that are placed for products in each category. Percent is the amount that the orders for that category contributed to the overall total of orders.





You use the One-Way Frequencies task to generate frequency tables from your data. You can also perform binomial and chi-square tests by using this task.

To generate crosstabulation (contingency) tables, you can use the Table Analysis task. A crosstabulation table summarizes for two or more classification variables by showing the number of observations for each combination of variable values.

Let's get started on your frequency report. To create it, you'll use the One-Way Frequencies task.
