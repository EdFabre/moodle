t-Tests

Overview

A t-test can be used to

  • compare a sample mean to a known value
  • compare the means of two related samples, or of the same sample at two points in time
  • compare the means of two independent samples.

The paired-sample t-test is used when you have two related measurements. A two-sample t-test, or independent t-test, is used when you have two independent sets of measurements.

Suppose researchers at a concrete company are interested in determining whether an additive in the mixing process affects the strength of concrete. Each batch of concrete was measured for strength by two operators. The company is also interested in whether the measurements by the two operators are consistent.

The data for the study is stored in the Concrete data table, which has the following variables:


Variable Description
Strength1 measured strength of concrete by operator 1 (the primary operator on duty)
Strength2 measured strength of concrete by operator 2 (the trainee on duty)
Brand brand of concrete (Graystone, Consolidated, EZ Mix)
Additive presence of reinforcement material (reinforced, standard)
Humidity observed humidity


The analysis focuses on two research questions and uses two types of t-test:

  • Are there differences between operators in strength measures? (A paired-samples t-test is used.)

  • Does the type of additive have any effect on concrete strength? (An independent t-test is used.)
