Two-Way Analysis of Variance (continued) View and interpret the linear models analyses.

Now that you know that the residuals are normally distributed, you go back and evaluate the results of the linear models analysis.

  1. Open or activate the results for the Linear Models analysis. The overall model is statistically significant.


Click to view the full window.


  1. Look at the tests for the individual effects. There is a significant main effect of Additive and a significant main effect of Brand, but the Brand*Additive interaction is not statistically significant.


Click to view the full window.


  1. Because Brand is a three-level variable, you must look to the means comparisons to interpret the main effect. Scroll down to the report for Brand. With the very conservative Bonferroni adjustment, only the difference between the Consolidated and Graystone concrete brands is significant.


Click to view the full window.


  1. It is not appropriate to interpret the LS means comparisons for the interaction because the interaction term in the model was not significant. However, it may be useful to look over the means plot to see what pattern the means take on. It is clear from the means plot that the effects of Additive do not depend to any great extent on the brand of concrete.
