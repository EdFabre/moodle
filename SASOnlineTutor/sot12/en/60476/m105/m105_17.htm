t-Tests (continued) View and evaluate the results.

In this task, you evaluate the results of the paired t-test. Then, you rename the analysis in the Process Flow window.

  1. The first table in the report shows descriptive statistics for the difference between Strength1 and Strength2 and 95% confidence interval bounds. The confidence interval includes 0; therefore, you can conclude that the measures collected by the two operators are not significantly different from one another.


Click to view the full window.


  1. A more direct result is given by the t-test report. This analysis shows a t-value of 0.72 with 29 degrees of freedom. The p-value is greater than our alpha level of .05, so we conclude that the strength measures for the operators are not significantly different. Because the confidence interval is based on a t-statistic, the results for the t-test report and the 95% confidence interval are mathematically equivalent at alpha = .05.




  1. To keep the reports organized, rename the t-test portion of the project tree. Right-click t test and select Rename. Type the name Paired t test for operator differences.

  2. Close the results windows.
