Analysis of Covariance

Overview

Consider a situation where you want to study the influence of a categorical predictor on a continuous response variable. You also need to be aware of possible differences in your response variable due to a continuous predictor. Before you compare the levels of the categorical predictor, you must adjust for the differences in the levels of the continuous predictor. This analysis is called an analysis of covariance (ANCOVA).

Suppose a lumber company has conducted an experiment to compare the tensile strength of five types of particle board. The tensile strength of a board may be affected by the specific density, which cannot be controlled. You can account for boards differing in specific density by including the specific density in the analysis as a continuous variable. The data from the experiment is stored in the Boards data table, which has the following variables.


Variable Description
Type type of particle board (A,B,C,D,E)
Density specific density of the board
Strength tensile strength of the board
