One-Way Analysis of Variance

Overview

To test for mean differences when there are two or more groups, you can use analysis of variance (ANOVA). ANOVA compares the portion of variability in the response variable attributable to the grouping variable to the portion of variability left unexplained.

The null hypothesis for one-way ANOVA is that all means are equal. The alternative hypothesis is that at least one mean is different from one other mean. Small differences between means are always present. The objective is to determine whether these differences are significant.
