Control Charts for Measurement Data (continued) View and evaluate the results.

In this task, you view and evaluate the results of the Control Charts task and examine the statistics saved in the output data table.

The charts are labeled with the variables, sample size, and control limits. Notice that Test 6 exceeds the upper control limits for the mean, while Test 7 exceeds the upper control limits for the range. No other signals were detected.




Click to view the full window.


The control limits and the subgroup statistics are saved in a separate data table. You can look over this table and identify samples that are out of range or that have signaled one of the eight tests for special causes.

  1. In the Project window, double-click the output data set for the Mean and Range Chart task you just ran.

  2. Look at the variables _EXLIM_ and _EXLIMR_. These variables indicate which samples are out of control for the mean and the range, respectively. As you can see, Sample 6 and Sample 7 are beyond the upper control limits for the mean and the range, respectively.
