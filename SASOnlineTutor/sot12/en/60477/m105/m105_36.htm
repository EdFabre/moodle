Two-Way Analysis of Variance (continued) Specify post-hoc tests.

In this task, you specify effects and options for the analysis.

  1. Click the Post-Hoc Tests tab.

  2. Click Add Effects. In the Select Effects to Use window, shift-click all three effects on the left and click the right arrow to add them to the analysis. Click OK.


Click to view the full window.


  1. Under Options for least-squares, select Show p-values for differences. In the field to the right, click the drop-down arrow and select All pairwise differences from the list. (The default option and the All pairwise differences option produce the same results in this case.)


Click to view the full window.


  1. In order to control the Type I error rate associated with multiple comparisons, some statistical adjustment is necessary. The default in SAS Enterprise Guide is to report t-tests with no Type I error adjustment. Select Adjustment method for comparison and then select Bonferroni from the drop-down menu to the right.


Click to view the full window.


There are many options available for controlling experimentwise Type I error rate, and Bonferroni is one of the more conservative of these. You may wish to use a less conservative method, such as the Tukey-Kramer HSD.
