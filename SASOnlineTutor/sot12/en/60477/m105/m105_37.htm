Two-Way Analysis of Variance (continued) Specify plots and predictions and run the analysis.

In this task, you select the plots you want to generate. Then you run the analysis.

  1. Click the Plots tab. Be sure the Means button at the top is selected. Select Dependent means for two-way effects to get a plot of the means.


Click to view the full window.


  1. To assist in evaluating the assumptions of linear models, you may want to look at the residuals. Click the Residual button, then select Ordinary vs predicted Y.


Click to view the full window.


There are many other plots you can request, such as leverage information to detect the effect of influential points.

  1. Click the Predictions tab. Under Data to predict, select Original sample. Under Save output data, select Predictions and Diagnostic statistics. Under Additional statistics, select Residuals.


Click to view the full window.

Click to view the full window.


Saving diagnostic statistics creates a new data table with the diagnostics ready for analysis. You can examine properties such as normality of residuals, influential points, PRESS statistics (for goodness of fit/observations that conform poorly to the model), and so on.

  1. Click Finish.
