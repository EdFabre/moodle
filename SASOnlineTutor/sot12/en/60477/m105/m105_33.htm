Two-Way Analysis of Variance

Overview

General linear models (also called linear models) refer to statistical models that assume linear relationships among one or more continuous or categorical predictors and one or more continuous responses. Linear models can provide a powerful approach to examining the relationships among variables.

In this example, you use the Concrete data set to analyze a 2 x 3 factorial ANOVA. A two-way ANOVA uses Brand and Additive as the independent variables and Strength1, the strength measured by operator 1, as the dependent variable. The variables in the Concrete data set are shown below.


Variable Description
Strength1 measured strength of concrete by operator 1 (the primary operator on duty)
Strength2 measured strength of concrete by operator 2 (the trainee on duty)
Brand brand of concrete (Graystone, Consolidated, EZ Mix)
Additive presence of reinforcement material (reinforced, standard)
Humidity observed humidity
